Orange wine is an alcoholic beverage produced through the fermentation process of orange juice with the help of microorganisms such as yeast (specifically Saccharomyces cerevisiae). This process converts the sugar content in orange juice into alcohol and other compounds that give orange wine its distinctive aroma and taste.

In its manufacture, oranges are peeled and squeezed to extract the juice, then mixed with sugar and yeast, then fermented in a closed container for several weeks to months. This fermentation process produces wine with varying alcohol content depending on the type of sugar used and the length of fermentation. The resulting orange wine has a distinctive sweet and sour taste of oranges and a fresh aroma, and can be stored longer than fresh fruit.
